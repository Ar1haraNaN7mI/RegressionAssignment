Group Regression Project


You are hired by American Cancer Society (https://www.cancer.org/) to write a white paper exploring the factors related to cancer deaths in the US. They will use your analysis to identify regions (and associated partners) for cancer interventions across the US. The dataset provided includes information about all zipcodes in the United States.

Create a model of incidence rate (Column K/ C11) using the factors in this dataset. In the white paper you should also write an executive summary of cancer factors in America, which can be read by non-statistical business decision makers. 

Specifically answer following questions:

1. Which regions of the country (Column D/ C4-T) are most prone to cancer? (Hint: Descriptive statistics)

2. Conduct hypothesis tests to determine if the following lead to more cancer incidence Use alpha of 5%. (Hint: Hypothesis test on population means, two categories)
a. Recent trend stable vs falling (Column M/ C13-T)
b. Study Count (Column C/ C3). Create a new variable to recode this As High vs Low and do the hypothesis test. Break the two groups at the mean study count.

3. Create a 4 level indicator variable for Median Income (Column G/ C7) and conduct an ANOVA comparing the four groups. Four levels might be Very low, low, high, very high or similar (Hint: Hypothesis test on population means, three or more categories)

4. Do a correlation analysis to see which factors are most highly correlated with cancer incidence rate (Column K/ C11) (and which are correlated with each other!) (Hint: Consider continuous variables)

5. Create an actionable regression model for incidence rate (Column K/ C11) and death rate (Column P), separately. Each regression model should explain as much of the variability as possible but only identify the most important factors. (Hint: R square, p-value, factor coefficient)

6. EXPLAIN your findings so that a non-technical person can understand!
